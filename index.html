1
The medical system waits for symptoms
Organ disease doesn't work that way...
▾

Every major organ imaging tool — ultrasound, CT, MRI — is triggered by symptoms the patient already has. The system waits for you to feel sick before it looks inside you. But liver fibrosis, kidney deterioration, and cardiac stiffening each progress for 5–15 years before any detectable symptom appears.

By the time you find out, the damage is often permanent. The window for reversible intervention has closed. SOMA exists to keep that window open.

2
Imaging is episodic and inaccessible by design
Even in wealthy countries, organ imaging is gated...
▾

A clinical ultrasound machine costs $5K–$100K. A CT scanner costs $500K–$2M. Even in wealthy countries, organ imaging is gated behind clinical referral and specialist access — meaning you typically get one scan every several years, triggered by a symptom you already have.

SOMA breaks this entirely: nightly monitoring, no operator required, no clinical visit needed.

3
The reversible window is open for years. Nobody knows it.
F1–F2 liver fibrosis is fully reversible. F4 is not. Most people are diagnosed at F4.
▾

Liver fibrosis stages F0–F2 are fully reversible with lifestyle changes. F3 is partial. F4 (cirrhosis) is largely irreversible. The average time from F0 to F4 is 10–20 years — entirely asymptomatic. The same pattern holds for kidney disease and cardiac fibrosis. SOMA targets the entire reversible window — when catching disease still changes the outcome.

The technology stack

01
Acoustic transducer array — Hardware
MEMS piezoelectric transducers in a flexible PDMS belt...
▾

A flexible belt embeds MEMS piezoelectric transducers in a PDMS (soft silicone) substrate. A miniaturised mechanical actuator generates low-frequency shear waves at 50–200 Hz — the same frequency range as FibroScan, the FDA-cleared clinical standard. The waves propagate through soft tissue toward the liver.

A micro-actuator pressure regulation system maintains consistent coupling geometry regardless of sleep position — this is the key hardware challenge and where the team's robotics background applies directly.

MEMS piezoelectric transducersPDMS flexible substrate50–200 Hz shear wave generationMicro-actuator pressure control
02
Shear wave velocity engine — Signal processing
Measures how fast waves travel through liver tissue...
▾

A pulse-echo ultrasound detector tracks how fast shear waves propagate through liver tissue. Faster velocity means stiffer tissue — and stiffer tissue means more fibrosis. The governing equation is E = 3ρcs²: tissue stiffness derived from shear wave speed and tissue density.

All computation runs on-device via edge inference. Your raw acoustic data never leaves the belt. The stiffness value (in kilopascals, kPa) maps directly to METAVIR fibrosis staging.

Acoustic CT reconstructionTime-of-flight analysisEdge inference on-device
03
Personal baseline AI — The core innovation
LSTM autoencoder learns your individual baseline over 30 nights...
▾

Every existing clinical tool compares your stiffness against a population average threshold. SOMA compares you against yourself. Over 30 nights, an LSTM autoencoder learns your personal organ stiffness baseline. It then monitors for deviation from that individual pattern.

If your personal baseline is 6.2 kPa, a reading of 6.9 kPa is a +11% personal deviation — flagged. Under the population model, it would never be noticed. This is SOMA's core scientific differentiation.

Step by Step

How a SOMA night works

1
You wear SOMA and fall asleep
No active effort. No setup ritual.
▾
The PDMS belt conforms to your abdomen. A micro-actuator pressure system maintains contact through sleep movement. Bioadhesive hydrogel coupling — pioneered by the MIT/USC BAUS-E (2024) project — ensures acoustic signal fidelity at the skin interface regardless of position.
2
Shear waves are generated at 50–200 Hz
The same physics as FibroScan — the FDA-cleared clinical standard.
▾
Unlike conventional ultrasound, SOMA generates shear waves — transverse mechanical vibrations that travel through tissue at 1–5 m/s. These are 1,000× slower than pressure waves, and far more sensitive to tissue mechanical properties. Stiffer tissue propagates them faster. This relationship is the entire physical basis of SOMA.
3
Wave velocity is measured, stiffness calculated
On-device edge AI converts wave speed into a kPa value.
▾
FibroScan — using identical physics — achieves ICC = 0.98 reproducibility and AUROC 0.89–0.99 for cirrhosis. All computation runs locally on the belt. The resulting liver stiffness value in kPa maps directly to METAVIR fibrosis stage. No data leaves the device.
4
Your personal AI baseline is updated
30-night LSTM model learns your individual pattern.
▾
Each night's reading updates your personal stiffness model. The LSTM autoencoder learns temporal patterns — not just average values but how your stiffness varies night-to-night with food, exercise, hydration, and sleep quality. The model distinguishes meaningful deviation from normal biological variation.
5
You wake up with a nightly report
All clear, or prompted for clinical follow-up.
▾
If your stiffness is tracking normally — all clear. If a statistically significant deviation is detected — SOMA prompts you to book a hepatology appointment. SOMA is not a diagnostic. It is a monitoring and early-warning system. The FDA pathway is Class II 510(k) as a diagnostic aid, citing FibroScan K143208 as predicate device.
The Physics

One equation governs everything SOMA does

E = 3ρcs²
E = Young's modulus — tissue stiffness (kPa) ρ = tissue density ≈ 1,060 kg/m³ (close to water) cs = shear wave velocity (m/s) — what SOMA measures
Healthy liver
cs ≈ 1.0–1.5 m/s → E ≈ 3–7 kPa
Early fibrosis (F2)
cs ≈ 1.7–2.0 m/s → E ≈ 8–12 kPa
Cirrhosis (F4)
cs ≈ 2.5–4.0 m/s → E ≈ 19–48 kPa

Source: Sandrin L. et al. "Transient elastography: a new noninvasive method for assessment of hepatic fibrosis." Ultrasound Med Biol. 2003;29(12):1705–13. · PMID 14698338  ·  Castera L. et al. Gastroenterology 2005;128(2):343–50. · PMID 15685546

Liu et al. published "Wearable Bioadhesive Ultrasound Shear Wave Elastography" (BAUS-E) in Science Advances (Feb 2024) — a postage-stamp-sized bioadhesive sticker with 128 acoustic channels that continuously monitored liver stiffness in live rats for 48 hours, detecting acute liver failure within 6 hours.

SOMA is not claiming to be first to demonstrate wearable acoustic elastography. BAUS-E proved the concept works. SOMA applies it to the preventive consumer market with a personal baseline AI — the layer that doesn't exist anywhere yet.

C
Sleep-time measurement — structural accuracy advantage
Food inflates stiffness 20–40% for 2–3 hours post-meal...
▾

FibroScan requires a 2–3 hour fast before each measurement — a requirement clinical guidelines note is "often overlooked in practice." Food intake increases liver stiffness by 20–40% for 2–3 hours post-meal via increased portal blood flow.

SOMA measures during sleep — naturally 3+ hours post-meal in most people. This eliminates the most common source of false positive results in clinical elastography. The sleep design isn't just convenient — it's structurally more accurate.

FAQ

Answers to the hard questions

How is SOMA different from FibroScan?
+
FibroScan is a clinical device requiring a trained operator and a hospital visit. It produces one stiffness reading compared against a population threshold. SOMA is worn during sleep, requires no operator, measures every night, and compares you against your own personal baseline — detecting earlier-stage deviation that population-norm tools miss.
Does sound at 50–200 Hz actually reach the liver?
+
Yes. Acoustic attenuation in soft tissue is ≈0.5 dB/cm/MHz. At 50–200 Hz, attenuation is near-zero — orders of magnitude less than clinical ultrasound. FibroScan operates at exactly 50 Hz and reaches the liver at 25–65mm depth. This is validated physics, not theoretical.
What about movement during sleep?
+
SOMA's micro-actuator maintains consistent coupling pressure and a signal quality gating algorithm only records measurements when acoustic coupling meets threshold. BAUS-E (2024) demonstrated 48-hour continuous monitoring in live organisms — movement tolerance is a solved engineering problem at the research stage.
Can SOMA detect early F1 fibrosis?
+
FibroScan achieves AUROC 0.77–0.87 for F1–F2 — adequate but not excellent. SOMA's personal baseline model is designed to improve on this by detecting your personal deviation before absolute values cross population cutoffs. This theoretical advantage is grounded in published AI methodology but requires clinical validation.
When will SOMA be available?
+
SOMA is a moonshot concept in development at TKS 2026. Year 1 is clinical proof of concept. Year 4 is the target for consumer launch, contingent on FDA 510(k) clearance. Join the waitlist to be first to know.
